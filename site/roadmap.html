MVP → Commercial

Roadmap

The MVP is a functional parallel-run pilot. Almost every deferral that made it shippable becomes a requirement for commercial use. The roadmap is structured as four tracks ordered by what unblocks revenue, not by what is easiest to build.

Track 1

Regulatory & Legal

The highest-lead-time item and the gate for everything downstream. Whether the platform's price-forming multi-dealer auction requires venue registration is an open question being taken to securities counsel — the answer depends on the instrument mix and cannot be determined without a specialist legal ruling. This track cannot be accelerated by engineering; it starts with counsel, not code.

Longest lead time · gates Track 3 venue work

Track 2

Security & Trust

No institutional buy-side firm approves a vendor without a credible security posture. This track covers SOC 2 Type II (observation window starts immediately), third-party penetration testing, secrets management, auth hardening, and demonstrable tenant isolation. It runs in parallel with Track 1 because both are calendar-bound in ways engineering is not.

Runs in parallel with Track 1 · gates enterprise procurement

Track 3

Product Depth

Real STP connectivity, configurable multi-firm approval chains, FX-normalized exposure, and full multi-tenant onboarding — the documented gaps the pilot deferred. Sequenced by what the first paying design partner actually blocks on, not by what is tractable to build. STP and governance specs are held pending the Track 1 ruling, since a venue classification changes their requirements.

Partially gated by Track 1 · sequenced by design-partner pull

Track 4

Scale & Operations

Observability, incident response, disaster recovery, and database load validation. A regulated venue handling real trade data needs an operational posture, not just a green deploy. This track must precede revenue dependence, but does not gate the pilot or a partner demo.

Before revenue dependence · does not gate pilot
